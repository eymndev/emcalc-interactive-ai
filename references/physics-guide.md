# E=mc² and Physics Reference Guide

This reference provides deeper physical context for the `emcalc_analyzer` skill when interpreting the outputs of the `emcalc` program.

## 1. Constants
- **C (Speed of Light)**: $299,792,458$ m/s
- **$C^2$**: $8.987551787 \times 10^{16}$ $m^2/s^2$

## 2. Real-World Conversion Efficiencies
When analyzing inputs, refer to these real-world equivalents:
- **Nuclear Fission**: Typically converts about 0.1% of nuclear mass into energy.
- **Nuclear Fusion**: Converts about 0.7% of mass into energy (e.g., hydrogen to helium).
- **Matter-Antimatter Annihilation**: The only process theoretically capable of 100% (1.0) mass-to-energy conversion efficiency.

## 3. Real-World Thermal-to-Electric Efficiencies (Joule to Electric)
- **Coal Power Plant**: ~33% to 40%
- **Nuclear Power Plant**: ~33% to 35%
- **Combined Cycle Gas Turbine**: ~50% to 60%

## 4. Energy Scale Context
To put the generated energy into perspective for users:
- **1 Joule**: Energy needed to lift a small apple ($100$g) by 1 meter.
- **1 kWh = $3.6 \times 10^6$ Joules**: Enough to run an average oven for about 30 minutes.
- **1 Megaton of TNT**: $\sim 4.18 \times 10^{15}$ Joules. (The energy contained in roughly 46.5 grams of perfect mass conversion).
